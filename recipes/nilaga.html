Beef Nilaga

Description

For a dish you can directly translate into English with the phrase “boiled beef,” you probably wouldn’t expect Nilagang Baka to be a delicious balance of flavors. From the soft and satisfying texture of baby bok choy to the ever tender and rich cubes of beef sirloin, this recipe only intermingles the best ingredients for an ever comforting dish. Perhaps one of the reasons why it is so popular is because like many other famous Filipino recipes, it puts together a good deal of meat, vegetables and stew.

Ingredients

  • 4 ½ pounds beef short ribs
  • 1 quart water
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 beef bouillon cubes
  • 2 carrots, cut in chunks
  • 2 stalks celery, quartered
  • 1 chayote squash, peeled and quartered
  • 2 potatoes, quartered
  • salt to taste
  • ¼ head cabbage, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Place the beef ribs in a large pot with enough water to cover.
  2. Bring to a boil over high heat, and boil for 5 minutes.
  3. Drain; discard the cooking water (the ribs will not be fully cooked).
  4. Return the beef to the pot. Pour in the 1 quart water, peppercorns, onions, and beef bouillon.
  5. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce the heat and simmer for 1 hour.
  6. Skim any fat floating on the surface. Stir in the carrots, celery and potatoes.
  7. Cover and simmer until potatoes are tender, about 20 minutes. Season with salt.
  8. Add cabbage and cook an additional 5 minutes.
